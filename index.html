A juxtapozíció

Latin eredetű szó, jelentése: mellérendelés, társítás. A kép és az azzal párhuzamosan elhangzó szöveg önkényes társítását értjük alatta. A nézői sztereotípiákra, előítéletekre alapoz. Példa: Ha egy bűntényről szóló narráció alatt roma embereket mutatunk, a néző úgy veszi, az elkövetők romák voltak még akkor is, ha ilyesmi nem hangzik el. Vagy: Egy korrupcióról szóló cikk környékére odatesszük XY politikus képét, az olvasó úgy veszi, hogy az illető korrupt − még akkor is, ha a cikk nem is említi az illetőt.

Grey

As we have said, when Richter was working on his project for Münster he replaced colored mirrors with grey mirrors. This is hardly surprising, because although there are a number of monochrome paintings in Richter’s oeuvre, he has always kept his distance from the notion of monochromaticism as an artistic category in its own right, which certain painters signed up to in the 1980s. Richter never had any interest in monochromaticism as a celebration of colour or as the last throes of painting; monochromaricism only had meaning for him insofar as it depicted something. Thus the individual hues in his colour charts, such as Ten Colours (1966), are not autonomous phenomena but rather neutral samples, and in the case of the coloured mirrors mentioned earlier here. Richter used those monochromes as an ambivalent vehicle for reflecting viewers and space. Richter also started to work on his Grey Paintings in 1966- not as a continuation of his previous use of black-and –white photographs as the basis of oil paintings, but as representations of the colour grey. The first of these grey paintings were called Two Greys Juxtaposed, Two Greys, One upon the Other, and Three Greys, One upon the Other.
